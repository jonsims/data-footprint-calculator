Sources & Methodology

Energy estimates represent total system cost: device + network + data center.

Google Search: 0.0003 kWh/query

Google Environmental Report (2024); corroborated by IEA and Carbon Brief.

AI Query: 0.003 kWh/query

de Vries, A. (2023). "The growing energy footprint of artificial intelligence." Joule, 7(10). The "10x Google" figure. More recent estimates (Epoch AI, 2025) suggest ~0.0003–0.001 kWh for optimized models like GPT-4o. The higher figure is used here to represent loaded queries (multi-turn, reasoning, image generation).

Video Streaming: 0.15 kWh/hour

IEA (2020) estimated ~0.077 kWh/hr; Carbon Brief reports up to 0.15 kWh/hr for HD on laptop/TV. Upper-middle estimate used.

Video Calls: 0.15 kWh/hour

Obringer et al. (2021), Purdue/Yale/MIT. Camera-on HD group calls at the higher end of 0.05–0.17 kWh/hr range.

Music Streaming: 0.02 kWh/hour

Constellation Energy (2020). Audio uses ~50x less bandwidth than HD video.

Cloud Sync: 0.06 kWh/GB

Modern hyperscale estimates range 0.01–0.10 kWh/GB; 0.06 is a midpoint for upload/download + short-term storage overhead.

Note on AI energy: The 0.003 kWh figure matches the course slides and the "10x Google search" framing. In 2025, OpenAI disclosed ~0.00034 kWh per average GPT-4o query. The range is wide because query complexity varies enormously.
